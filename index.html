Selected Projects

Non-convex Min-max Optimization and GANs
ongoing

Traditionally a GAN model is trained by gradient descent ascent (GDA) method, where a discriminator only makes local updates. It is very common that in GAN training, a generator only produce one output to fool the discriminator, which is called mode collapse. In this project we first discover the mode collapse on a 0-1 MNIST dataset and a synthetic gaussian mixture dataset. Currently we are working on developing high order methods for non-convex min-max optimization.

Tensor Methods: Theoretical Advances, Challenges and Applications
Master thesis, ongoing

I implemented the second-order and third-order tensor methods, as well as their accelerated versions, proposed in this paper. I tested these methods on different problems and in practice, the third-order method is superior to the second-order method. Currently, I am working on applying cubic regularized Newton's method in non-convex min-max optimization, especially in GAN training.

GAN-UNET: Non-negative Matrix Factorization Using Deep Neural Networks
Deep Learning Course Project, Spring 2020

We introduced an end-to-end deep neural network framework GAN-UNET for non-negative matrix factorization (NNMF). We tested our model on different datasets to assess the performance in terms of reconstruction error, compared with many other classic algorithms. We also discussed the ability of symmetry breaking of our model from a perspective of inverse problems.

Regional Co-location Pattern Detection
Spatial Data Science Course Project, Spring 2020

We introduced a new algorithm called Unique Quadruplet Enumeration algorithm, which outperforms our baseline significantly in experimentation with both synthetic data and a real world crime. We also introduced a number of new pruning metrics that lay the ground work to developing even better algorithms in future.

Assistant Decision-making System of Maritime Search and Rescue
National Undergraduate Innovation and Entrepreneurship Training Program, Summer 2017

In this project, we presented a new algorithm based on 2-D spatial grid interpolation to locate the drowning people with real-time radar data. We simulated the algorithm and plotted the trajectory in MatLab. Besides, we also developed a website in PHP to demonstrate the trajectory of people using BaiduMap API.
